WHAT IS TEXTILE PRINTING ?

Textile printing is the process of applying color to fabric in definite patterns or designs. In properly printed fabrics the colour is bonded with the fibre, so as to resist washing and friction. Textile printing is related to dyeing but in dyeing properly the whole fabric is uniformly covered with one colour, whereas in printing one or more colours are applied to it in certain parts only, and in sharply defined patterns.

BRIEF HISTORY OF TEXTILE PRINTING

Textile printing has undergone a fascinating evolution over the centuries, starting with traditional hand methods like block printing and stenciling. The Industrial Revolution brought about mechanical printing machines in the 18th century, while the 20th century saw the rise of screen printing and later, digital printing technologies. From the intricate copperplate engravings to the continuous high-speed rotary screen printing, each era contributed to the industry's efficiency and design capabilities. Today, digital printing dominates, offering unparalleled flexibility and customization. Sustainability and smart textile integration have become key considerations, marking the latest chapters in the ever-evolving history of textile printing.
